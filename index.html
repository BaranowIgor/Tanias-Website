FAQ

What is the difference between Botox and dermal fillers?

Botox and dermal fillers are both injection treatments, but they serve different purposes. Botox is used to relax facial muscles and reduce wrinkles, while dermal fillers add volume to areas like the cheeks and lips to restore youthful contours.

Can aesthetic cosmetology treatments help with acne-prone skin?

Yes, many aesthetic treatments, such as chemical peels and laser therapies, can be effective in reducing acne and improving the overall texture and tone of the skin.

What is a HydraFacial, and what are its benefits?

A HydraFacial is a multi-step facial treatment that cleanses, exfoliates, and hydrates the skin. It can improve skin texture, reduce fine lines, and give your skin a radiant glow.

How does radiofrequency skin tightening work?

Radiofrequency skin tightening uses energy to heat the deeper layers of the skin, stimulating collagen production and tightening the skin. It can be used on areas like the face, neck, and body to reduce sagging and improve elasticity.

Are injectable treatments painful?

Most injectable treatments involve minimal discomfort, as practitioners often use fine needles and can apply a numbing cream to the treatment area for added comfort.

How long do the effects of dermal fillers last?

The longevity of dermal fillers varies depending on the type of filler used and individual factors. Generally, results can last anywhere from 6 months to 2 years.

Can I combine different injection treatments during one session?

In many cases, combining treatments like Botox and dermal fillers can be done in the same session to address multiple concerns effectively. Your provider will guide you based on your specific needs.

Can I wear makeup after a chemical peel?

It's best to avoid wearing makeup for at least 24 hours after a chemical peel to allow the skin to heal properly.

Are there any side effects of microdermabrasion?

Microdermabrasion is generally safe, but it may cause temporary redness or mild sensitivity. These effects typically subside within a few hours after the treatment.
